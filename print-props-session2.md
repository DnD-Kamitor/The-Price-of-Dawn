## Player Moments

<details>
<summary>Tomas — The Long Preparation [player of Tomas's closest PC only]</summary>

*Play this when Tomas arrives mid-revelation at the Dawnhalls.*

---

He reads the room before he reaches you. You can see him doing it — the way someone reads a situation who has been expecting this situation for a long time.

He sits down. He asks three questions. Each one is precise. Each one suggests he already knows the general shape of the answer and is confirming the details.

After the third question, he is quiet for a moment.

Then: *"I saw a secondary notation in a ritual diagram once. About seven years ago — a copy of a copy, badly transcribed. I didn't know what I was reading at the time. I filed it as 'notation anomaly, unclear.' I looked for the original twice. Never found it."*

He looks at the diagram in your hands.

*"I think I found it now."*

He does not look afraid. He looks like someone who has been carrying a low-grade dread for a very long time and has just been told the thing he was dreading is real. That is, in a specific way, a relief.

*What do you say to him?*

</details>

<details>
<summary>Lira — The Hard No [player of Lira's closest PC only]</summary>

*Play this at the branching point, when Lira is told.*

---

She goes still first. Not cold — still. The way someone goes still when they need a moment before they respond because the response that is coming without the moment would be the wrong one.

Then: *"No."*

She looks around the room. At Sera. At Petra. At the wall.

*"Find another way."*

She walks out.

She is not finished with this conversation. She is finished with it right now, in this room, with these people, while she is still deciding whether to trust any of you. She has a daughter the party may not know about yet. She has been saying no to the city's needs since she was nineteen years old and her name started appearing on Dawnborn registry documents.

She will come back. She will want to know if there is actually another way.

If someone follows her: she stops walking. She doesn't turn around. She says: *"Give me an hour. If you have found an alternative, it will still be true in an hour."*

*Do you follow her?*

</details>

<details>
<summary>Kaelen — Reading the Reckoning [Moustaffa only]</summary>

*Play this during either combat — Archive or Dawnhalls. No roll required.*

---

You've fought organised units before. You know the difference between zealots and soldiers.

The Reckoning fights like soldiers.

Not fanatically. Not sacrificially. They have assigned roles — one to restrain, one to strike, one to control the exit. They have a priority hierarchy: documents first, Theron second, the party third. When Marsh takes a hit he doesn't react emotionally. He recalculates.

These people have been training for this specific operation. Someone drilled them on the Archive layout. Someone told them where the restricted section was and what was in it.

The Reckoning isn't a cult running on ideology. It's a paramilitary unit running on an objective.

*Who gave them the objective? Who funded the training?*

You don't have an answer. But you know what a well-resourced military unit looks like. This is one.

</details>
