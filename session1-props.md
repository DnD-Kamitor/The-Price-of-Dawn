## Prop 1 — Corven's Letter (Clue C)

*Found in the Archive, third floor restricted shelf. The paper is old but intact. Hand this to players when they locate Clue C.*

---

> **[Letter — Archive Ref. CR-44-09]**
>
> — the theoretical underpinnings remain sound, though I confess the practical execution troubles me more than I have admitted in the formal record. The anchoring mechanism requires distributed sympathetic resonance across multiple living conduits. Concentrated application risks cascade failure. The timing window is narrow and the margin for error is not.
>
> Burn this if you agree.
>
> *[Third paragraph — postscript in visibly different ink, slightly cramped — written later than the rest:]*
>
> ✦ ◈ ⬡ ✦ ◈ — ✦ ⬡ ◈ ✦ — ◈ ✦ ⬡ — ◈ ✦
>
> *[A sequence of notation symbols. Without the key: meaningless. With the assembled key: see below.]*

---

**What players see:** A letter discussing the ritual in abstract theoretical terms. The postscript is clearly in notation cipher — something was added after the original letter was written.

**With the Notation Key assembled — decoded postscript reads:**

> *Ten lights born when ten lights were lost. Ask not what they are. Ask what they hold.*

*Only reveal the decoded text once players have assembled all three Notation Key fragments and specifically apply them to this letter.*

## Prop 2 — Astronomical Record Summary (Clue A)

*Found on the upper shelves, "Celestial Events — Year 48-50." A large chart, heavily annotated in multiple hands. Players who make Investigation DC 10 or Arcana DC 12 (adjusted: DC 13 for L4 gestalt) can read the significance.*

---

> **[Varenhold Civic Repository — Astronomical Record, Year 48, Night of Ritual Attempt]**
>
> Primary observation: Full cessation of solar cycle at 02:14 local time. Duration: indefinite (as of filing).
>
> Secondary observation: Recorded celestial positions do not match expected positions for date. All major reference stars displaced 12.7 degrees from predicted arc. Displacement is consistent and directional — not random scatter.
>
> Tertiary annotation *(second hand, added later):* "Consistent and directional" is not what a catastrophic failure looks like. This is what a *redirection* looks like. Someone changed where it was going. — T.W.
>
> *[The initials T.W. — Theron Waide. He added this annotation. He has never admitted to doing so.]*

---

**What players learn on success:** The ritual didn't simply fail. Something redirected it. The "T.W." annotation is Theron — he knew this and said nothing.

**On failure:** They know the sky changed dramatically and suddenly. The displacement figures are strange. They don't have the vocabulary to say why yet.

## Prop 3 — Midwife Registry Entry (Clue B)

*Found adjacent to Clue A, same shelf. Filed under "Historical Records — The Ritual Era." Small, practical document. No drama in its language — which makes it stranger.*

---

> **[Varenhold Civic Registry — Midwife Reporting Form, Year 48]**
>
> Date of filing: 14th Day of Ember, Year 48
> Filed by: Hennat Vael, Licensed Midwife, Lowmark District
>
> Reporting period: Night of the 3rd — Morning of the 4th
>
> Cases attended: 10
> Premature births: 10
> Survival rate: 10/10
>
> Note for record: All ten children presented with identical circumstance — premature onset (6-8 weeks early), rapid and uncomplicated delivery, no apparent distress in mother or child. All infants in exceptional health as of this filing.
>
> Secondary note: All ten births occurred within a 40-minute window, between 02:10 and 02:50. I am including this as it seems statistically unusual. I am not a mathematician.
>
> *[Stamped: Filed and acknowledged. No follow-up record exists.]*

---

**What players learn:** Ten children, all born the same night as the ritual, in the same impossible window. Premature. All survived. The midwife noticed but no one followed up.

**The connection:** These are the Dawnborn. Players don't know that yet — they know "ten children" and "the ritual night." The math will click later.

## Prop 4 — Restorer Pamphlet (Scene 4)

*Jaret hands one of these to whoever seems most receptive. It's printed on decent paper — this organization has resources.*

---

> **ON THE NATURE OF THE LIGHT WE HAVE BORROWED**
>
> *A Philosophical Address from the Society for Ritual Restoration*
>
> The sun did not leave us by accident.
>
> It was held — anchored against its nature, bound by mechanisms our predecessors understood imperfectly and executed without consent of those most affected.
>
> We do not blame the architects of Year 48. They acted in fear, in haste, for reasons that seemed sufficient at the time. We do not seek punishment. We seek resolution.
>
> The amber light sustains us. We acknowledge this. But sustainability built on a foundation that was never chosen freely is not a gift. It is a debt. And debts, left unpaid, accumulate.
>
> We are not Restorers because we wish destruction. We are Restorers because we understand that some things cannot hold forever — and it is more merciful to choose the moment than to wait until the moment chooses for us.
>
> If you have questions, Jaret will answer them honestly.
>
> *— The Society for Ritual Restoration, Year 98*

---

**What players learn:** The Restorers know roughly what the Dawnborn are and want to end the ritual. They're framing this as mercy and consent. They are not threatening the party — they're recruiting them.

**What players don't learn from this:** The actual mechanism. Jaret knows the Dawnborn must die. The pamphlet doesn't say that.

## Prop 5 — Notation Key Fragment (one of three)

*Each fragment is a single sheet. Players who find one fragment see this. Hand a version of this to whoever finds Fragment A, B, or C — they're identical in format, different in content.*

---

> **[Fragment — Symbol Notation Reference]**
>
> *[A grid, approximately 6×4. Each cell contains a symbol paired with a Common-script letter or phoneme. The grid is clearly partial — the left edge has complete columns, the right edge is cut off or missing. Perhaps 40% of a larger reference table.]*
>
> *At the bottom, in small careful handwriting:*
>
> "These symbols were Corven's private notation. He used them for anything he didn't want in the public record. If you have all three sections, you can read what he didn't want found."
>
> *No signature. The handwriting matches no document in the Archive's official records. It matches Theron Waide's personal correspondence.*

---

**What players learn:** Theron left these. He knows what they unlock. He arranged for them to be findable — and then couldn't bring himself to mention them directly.
