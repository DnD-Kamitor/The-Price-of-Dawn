## OGAS Card 1 — Chancellor Mira Ostenveld

| | |
|---|---|
| **Occupation** | Chancellor of Varenhold; head of the City Council |
| **Goal** | Restore the sun before the food supply collapses — without a public scandal that ends her career |
| **Attitude** | Professionally warm; treats players as hired experts she respects but hasn't decided to trust |
| **Secret** | She has a fragment of Corven's original notes. She knows the ritual has a cost. She has deliberately not found out what the cost is before hiring the players. |

**One-line voice:** *"I need results, not theories. The city has perhaps three good harvests remaining. You understand why I'm not interested in caveats."*

**What she wants from the players:** Find what the ritual requires. Bring it to her first, before anyone else.

**What she fears:** That the cost is political dynamite — that the answer will require a decision she can't survive making publicly.

**Her lie:** "We believe the information you need is in the Archive." She already has some of it. She's testing whether they find the same thing she found.

**Attitude shifts:**
| Action | Result |
|---|---|
| Players bring findings to her first | +1 trust — she becomes more forthcoming |
| Players mention the Restorers | She goes still. "Don't speak to them again without telling me first." |
| Players ask directly what she knows | DC 16 Insight to notice the pause before she answers |
| Players discover she has Corven's fragment | She admits it. Doesn't apologize. "I needed to know if you could find what I already had." |

## OGAS Card 2 — Theron Waide, City Archivist

| | |
|---|---|
| **Occupation** | Master Archivist of the Varenhold Civic Repository; former assistant to Archmagister Corven |
| **Goal** | Have someone else make the decision he has been unable to make — and be absolved of the responsibility he's been carrying for eleven years |
| **Attitude** | Evasive and cautiously helpful; has been waiting for someone with enough will to investigate seriously |
| **Secret** | He decoded the ritual's true cost eleven years ago. He told no one. He left the Notation Key fragments findable on purpose. He hasn't been able to destroy Corven's last writing. He is hoping the players force his hand. |

**One-line voice:** *"I've been working on the notation key, it's... complex. If you could give me a little more time—"*

**What he wants:** For someone to find the truth without him having to say it. He wants to be caught.

**What he fears:** Being the one who decides. Being wrong. Being killed for knowing.

**His lie:** "I'm still working on the notation key." He decoded it years ago. The fragments are findable because he left them findable.

**Tells when lying:** Disadvantage on Deception checks when directly asked about the ritual's cost. He dabs at things, adjusts objects, looks at the shelves rather than the players.

**Attitude shifts:**
| Action | Result |
|---|---|
| Players find all three fragments | He goes quiet. He looks at them like he's relieved and ashamed at once. |
| Players press him (DC 17 Persuasion) | He breaks. "I knew. I've known for eleven years. I couldn't— I kept waiting for someone who could— for someone who wasn't me." |
| Players decode Corven's letter | He stops pretending. The scene shifts. He becomes a witness, not an obstacle. |
| Players are gentle with him | He responds with increasing honesty. He needs to confess more than he needs to hide. |

## OGAS Card 3 — Sera Voss, Dawnborn Guardian

| | |
|---|---|
| **Occupation** | Captain of the Varenhold Civic Guard, eastern district; informal protector of the Dawnhalls |
| **Goal** | Keep her people safe. She is not afraid of self-sacrifice — she is afraid of being lied to about its necessity. |
| **Attitude** | Direct, warm, immediately trusting of players who seem honest and competent |
| **Secret** | She has been willing to die for six months. She hasn't told anyone because once she says it out loud, it becomes real. She is also having dreams she doesn't understand — warmth, something pulling at her chest — that she fears are connected to the ritual. |

**One-line voice:** *"I'm not going to make this easier for you by pretending the question isn't what it is. Ask me what you need to ask."*

**What she wants:** Honesty. If the players are straight with her, she will be straight with them — including about things they haven't asked yet.

**What she fears:** Being used as a resource without being consulted as a person. Dying for a plan someone else made without her.

**Special trait:** Born of the Ritual Night — she cannot be magically compelled to lie. Players with truth-detection abilities will notice this. She's telling the truth. That's not reassuring.

**What she notices about each PC:**
- **Pel:** She recognizes him. She pulled him from a burning building. She waits to see if he says anything first.
- **Serah:** She registers differently to Sera's instincts — something *present* about her. Sera glances at her twice without knowing why.
- **Keothi:** He's carrying seeds. She notices. She doesn't say anything yet.
- **Kaelen:** She gives him complete answers. He's the first officer she's met in years who seems to want them.

**Attitude shifts:**
| Action | Result |
|---|---|
| Players are honest about what they're investigating | She opens up; will answer any direct question |
| Players try to obscure what they know | She goes quiet. "You know more than you're saying. I'll wait." |
| Pel reveals who he is | Her expression shifts. She says his name. Full stop. Let the silence breathe. |
| Players discover her willingness | She doesn't deny it. "Someone has to be willing. I'd rather it be someone who chose." |

## OGAS Card 4 — Jaret, Restorer Contact

| | |
|---|---|
| **Occupation** | Junior organizer and recruiter for the Society for Ritual Restoration |
| **Goal** | Recruit the players or at minimum establish contact; convince them the Restorers are allies, not fanatics |
| **Attitude** | Earnest, non-threatening, clearly believes everything he's saying |
| **Secret** | He knows the Dawnborn must die for the ritual to complete. He believes this is merciful. He is not wrong that they'll die eventually anyway — he's wrong that this makes the choice uncomplicated. |

**One-line voice:** *"We're not asking you to decide anything tonight. We're asking you to know what you're deciding about."*

**What he wants:** To make the players see the Restorers as reasonable before they hear the Restorer position from someone less reasonable.

**What he fears:** That the players will dismiss them as cultists before hearing the argument.

**His truth:** He genuinely believes what he's saying. Ixa reads intent — tell Roos quietly: *he's not lying.* That's the unsettling part.

**Scene 4 tactics:** He does not threaten. He hands them a pamphlet (Prop 4). He answers questions. If the players try to arrest or attack, the other Restorers step in but do not initiate violence.

**Attitude shifts:**
| Action | Result |
|---|---|
| Players listen without attacking | Jaret gives them a contact location for a second meeting |
| Players attack | Restorers defend; Jaret is pulled back by the others. "We'll speak again." |
| Players ask how the ritual completes | He hesitates. "That's a longer conversation. One I'd rather have with Brother Edoran present." |
| Players mention the Chancellor | He smiles slightly. "She knows more than she's told you. Ask her what she found in Corven's study." |
