# Session 3 — Lesser Evils (Level 6)

*Dilemma: Is a "lesser evil" still evil? How far do you compromise?*

---

## Scene 1 — Opening Combat: Senna's Mob

**Five senses:**
- Sight: A Lowmark alley, narrow, blocked at both ends by bodies — parents, dock workers, people who look like they haven't slept.
- Sound: Not a roar. A crowd-murmur: overlapping names, a child crying somewhere behind the press.
- Smell: Woodsmoke from an overturned brazier, unwashed wool, spilled fish broth from a kicked market pot.
- Touch: The ambient cold of a street that never sees direct light. Something underfoot that crunches — broken crockery.
- Air: Still. No wind. The alley holds the crowd's heat.

**Setup:** Isolde Maren works out of a rented room above a chandler's shop on Lowmark Alley. When players arrive, thirty-odd Desperate faction members are blocking access to the building. Senna Kard stands at their center — dockworker's build, thirty-something, the particular exhaustion of someone awake for thirty hours straight. She is not here to fight. She is here because she is out of other options.

**Secondary Objective (state this before combat begins):** De-escalate without fighting. If even one Desperate member drops to 0 HP from a player's attack, the entire Desperate faction marks the party as Reckoning-adjacent — consequences carry to Sessions 4 and 5. The goal is to get through the crowd, not through the people.

**Enemies:**
- Senna Kard: 1, use Bandit Captain stats. Motivated by two daughters (Nia, 9; Brek, 6) who are showing grey sickness symptoms. She will not initiate violence unless players do.
- Desperate Members: 6, use Commoner stats with clubs (1d4 bludgeoning). Motivated by fear, not malice. Morale breaks when Senna stands down or when any two members fall.

**Combat ends when:** Players fight through (crowd breaks at 3 members downed, Senna surrenders at 5 HP) OR players de-escalate. De-escalation requires DC 13 Persuasion with a specific acknowledgment of Senna's loss — not "we understand your pain" but something that names what she's actually losing. Generic appeals fail automatically.

**What players learn:** If de-escalated, Senna steps aside and says: *"If they die for this, I need to know they chose it."* The consent question, from an unexpected source, before players have even met Isolde.

## Scene 2 — The Riddle: Isolde's Frequency Lock

**Setup:** Isolde built a resonance lock on her workroom door after Keseph's agents broke in twice. The door only opens to a specific sustained tone. A card is pinned to the door at eye level.

**The Riddle:**
> I am the same at the beginning and end of a prayer.
> I live between noise and music.
> You cannot hold me, but you can give me away completely.
> What am I?

**Answer:** A tone. Specifically: a sustained hum held at A above middle C (440 Hz). Any player who hums, sings, or describes producing a clear, sustained, unwavering note triggers it.

**Correct:** The lock resonates in sympathy and clicks open. Isolde's voice from inside, dry: "Finally."

**Wrong (first):** A discordant buzz. The door stays shut. Isolde's voice: "Closer. You're thinking about it wrong."

**Wrong (third):** The lock emits the dissonant tone at full volume — audible across the street. Passersby stop. Davan Crel, Keseph's aide, who has been watching from a cafe across the alley, now has the building's exact location. He will file a competing permit claim on this address within the hour.

**GM Note:** The answer — a tone you give away completely — mirrors what the Dawnborn are being asked to do. If players notice, let them sit with it.

## Scene 3 — The Puzzle: The Transfer Apparatus

**Setup:** Isolde's workroom is cramped and brilliant — three lux-crystal channels mounted on a workbench, each a different color (amber, silver, grey), surrounded by annotated diagrams and a shelf of specimen jars. One jar holds a grey mouse that moves wrong, like something walking through water. Players who make DC 14 Medicine or DC 13 Arcana understand what they're seeing before Isolde says anything: the mouse survived the transfer. It is not the mouse it was. This is the outcome she hasn't found a word for.

**The Puzzle:** Each channel has a direction dial calibrated 0°-360°. The correct configuration redirects lux energy without destroying the vessel. The three correct dial positions are: amber=60°, silver=180°, grey=300°. These correspond to the angular positions of the three surge-phase Dawnborn at the Ashring during the original ritual — derivable from Tomas's Asymmetry Journal (found or decoded in Session 2). Players with the journal get advantage on the DC 14 Arcana check to work it out; DC 12 with journal in hand and a successful DC 10 History check to connect the positions to the star map inside.

**Solution:** Set amber to 60°, silver to 180°, grey to 300°. Speak the configuration aloud to Isolde if uncertain — she can confirm or deny without giving the answer directly.

**Partial success:** Two of three channels correct: the energy redirect is unstable. Mortality risk for the Transfer path rises to 25% instead of 15-20%. Isolde: "Not wrong enough to stop. Wrong enough to matter."

**Failure:** Wrong configuration discharges 2d8 radiant damage in a 10-foot burst. Crystals reset to 0°. Three failed attempts without a rest: Isolde says the rebuild takes 72 hours, and the campaign clock advances one step.

**Success:** The apparatus produces a sustained amber resonance — the first stable result Isolde has achieved in months. She goes very still. Then: *"It works. Theoretically."* Players now have proof of concept to bring to the Dawnborn and to the Chancellor.

**Connected to:** Session 5 — if this apparatus is intact at Session 5, Ending C (Transfer) is a viable path. If it's destroyed in Scene 5 of this session, players must rebuild under deadline pressure before Session 5 closes.

## Scene 4 — The RP Scene: The Sera/Lira Confrontation

**NPC:** Sera Voss and Lira Anwick (two-NPC scene — run both)

**Sera wants:** Players to understand that some Dawnborn have already decided — freely, clearly, without coercion — to proceed with the ritual.

**Sera fears:** That her willingness will be used as a reason to pressure the unwilling.

**Lira wants:** Players to understand that some Dawnborn have also decided — freely, clearly, without coercion — to refuse.

**Lira fears:** That the existence of willing Dawnborn will make her refusal politically irrelevant.

**The lie:** Neither is lying. This is what makes the scene unresolvable.

**Setup:** Players bring the transfer apparatus news to the Dawnhall common room. Both women are there. Sera has just heard and is cautiously encouraged. Lira has just heard and is not. The argument begins before players can speak.

**Five senses:**
- Sight: Amber lantern light, long communal table, a child's drawing pinned to the wall near the kitchen door.
- Sound: The argument starts at conversation volume. It does not get loud. That's worse.
- Smell: Lowmark stew from the kitchen, warm bread, the particular smell of a room full of people who've been living in it for weeks.

**The Encounter Beat:** Lira says, quietly, to Sera: *"You want to die for them. I understand that. I have a daughter. You don't."* Sera does not answer. She has no answer. The two women look at each other across fifty years of friendship and the gap is visible. Do not resolve this. Let it be real. Players who intervene cannot fix it — they can only be present.

**Sample dialogue:**
- Sera (opening, to players): *"Someone has to make a decision. We can't wait for a perfect answer that might not exist."*
- Lira (when pushed on the transfer option): *"Fifteen percent chance I die anyway, and I lose whatever I am if I survive? That's supposed to be better?"*
- Lira (most honest): *"I have a daughter. That's the whole argument. I don't have another one."*

**How it connects forward:** Players now know the consent problem is not theoretical. Two Dawnborn, same room, diametrically opposite positions, both freely chosen. Any path that requires unanimous agreement must reckon with this scene.

## Scene 5 — Closing Combat: Reckoning Raids Isolde

**Setup:** It is night. Players are at or returning to Isolde's rented rooms. Reckoning soldiers tracked the players from the Dawnhall — Harran learned about the transfer apparatus through his Spire contact Varren Aldiss and has calculated that it gives the Dawnborn leverage to stall indefinitely. The apparatus must go.

**Secondary Objective (state before combat begins):** Keep the transfer apparatus intact. It sits on Isolde's workbench. If it takes 20 or more points of damage during the fight, it is destroyed and Scene 3's success is lost. Players must actively protect it — this means someone has to not be fighting.

**Enemies:**
- Reckoning Soldiers: 4, use Veteran stats (MM p.350). Professional, not fanatical. They spread out to prevent a clean defensive line.
- Reckoning Enforcer: 1, use Knight stats (MM p.347). Commands the soldiers, focuses attacks on whichever player is closest to the apparatus.

**Combat ends when:** The Enforcer is downed. Remaining soldiers disengage and withdraw — they do not fight without a commander present.

**Session hook:** Harran knew the apparatus existed. He knew where it was. He knew within hours of the players reaching it. Someone informed him. The most likely candidate is Davan Crel — Keseph's aide, who pinpointed the location when the frequency lock misfired. But players may suspect Varren, Fenwick, or anyone they've met. Take note of who they suspect. It matters.

## End of Session

**What players now know:**
- Three paths exist: Transfer (Isolde's apparatus), Asymmetry (Tomas's five surge-phase Dawnborn), and something hinted at by the Ashring stones that hasn't been fully revealed.
- The Reckoning is actively working to close down alternatives. Someone is feeding them information.
- Consent among the Dawnborn is not a single answer. It is ten separate answers, and they are not the same.

**What they carry forward:** Lira's face when she said *"I have a daughter."*

**Session debrief question:** *"If all the choices are bad, do you still have to choose? And if you don't — is that also a choice?"*
