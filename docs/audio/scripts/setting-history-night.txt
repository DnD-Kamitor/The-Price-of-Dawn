Before the twilight, Varenhold gleamed. The Council hired the best archmages of three kingdoms to perform the Ritual of Eternal Dawn—an audacious attempt to bottle sunlight so the city would never fear winter again. Families set tables in the plazas, merchants took bets on how bright the sky would be, and parents let their children stay awake to watch history happen.

History did happen. The ritual inverted. Dawn rose everywhere else in the world while Varenhold's horizon simply refused to move. The light stayed amber and sourceless, and the city realized within hours that something fundamental had broken. Archmagister Corven died in the backlash, taking most of his notes with him.

In the hospitals, ten premature labors started the moment the Ashring flared. All ten children lived. They healed fast, grew unsettlingly strong, sensed danger before anyone spoke it aloud. The city began calling them the Dawnborn long before scholars had a theory. They became guards, healers, negotiators, the kind of civic anchors a wounded city needed. For fifty years Varenhold has survived because those ten people stood in the gap the sun left behind.
