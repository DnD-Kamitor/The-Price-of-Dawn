**ON THE NATURE OF THE LIGHT WE HAVE BORROWED**
*A Philosophical Address from the Society for Ritual Restoration*

The sun did not leave us by accident.

It was held — anchored against its nature, bound by mechanisms our predecessors understood imperfectly and executed without the consent of those most affected.

We do not blame the architects of Year 48. They acted in fear, in haste, for reasons that seemed sufficient at the time. We do not seek punishment. We seek resolution.

The amber light sustains us. We acknowledge this. But sustainability built on a foundation that was never chosen freely is not a gift. It is a debt. And debts, left unpaid, accumulate.

We are not Restorers because we wish destruction. We are Restorers because we understand that some things cannot hold forever — and it is more merciful to choose the moment than to wait until the moment chooses for us.

If you have questions, Jaret will answer them honestly.

*— The Society for Ritual Restoration, Year 98*

*He is not a liar. He is someone who leads with the argument before the cost.*

## Player Moments

<details>
<summary>Pel — The Recognition [Frank's player only]</summary>

*Play this when Sera Voss first looks at Pel directly — whether or not Pel has said anything.*

---

She's been talking to the group. Giving clear, complete answers the way she always gives them — which you noticed immediately, because you have never once in your professional life received a complete answer from someone in authority.

She turns to you specifically. Not because you said anything. Because she looked at the group and then looked at you twice.

Then she says your name.

Not "you" or "the investigator" or "your colleague." Your name. First name. The one you stopped using when you started working criminal investigation, because it was safer to be a title than a person.

She remembers your name. You were a stranger in a burning building. You were unconscious for part of it. She didn't know you then and she doesn't know who you are now.

She says: *"I thought it might be you. When the Chancellor mentioned investigators, I thought — the probability of it was low. But."*

She doesn't finish that sentence.

*What do you do?*

</details>

<details>
<summary>Serah — Divine Sense Reading [Kirsten's player only]</summary>

*Play this when Serah uses Divine Sense in the Dawnhalls.*

---

You've used Divine Sense in the Chancellor's study: nothing. In the Archive: nothing. Standard Spire Quarter. Bureaucratic and unglamorous and theologically unremarkable.

The Dawnhalls is different.

No celestials. No fiends. No undead. Those are the categories your training gave you — the things it names, the presences it identifies. What you're feeling doesn't fit any of them.

Sera Voss is standing twelve feet away explaining the Dawnhalls' intake procedures to the rest of the group. She is not radiating anything dangerous or holy or corrupt.

She is radiating *presence*. Like standing near a hearth where the fire hasn't been lit yet. The stone is warm. Something is about to burn. It isn't burning now.

You have felt this once before — standing at a wayshrine, very young, in the rain. The Wanderer's shrines feel like that. Like something absent that is still somehow there.

Sera Voss is not a wayshrine.

You don't know what she is. Write it down somewhere. You will need to remember this feeling later.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Keothi — The Seeds [Dennis's player only]</summary>

*Play this when Keothi is in the Dawnhalls kitchen or offers the seeds to anyone.*

---

The cook's name is Mira. Not the Chancellor — a different Mira. She's been in this kitchen for nine years, since the last head cook left and nobody qualified came to replace her, so she stayed.

She hands you a wooden spoon while you're standing near the prep area. Not a question. Not an invitation. Just: here, you look like you know what to do with this. You do.

She tells you, while you work, that they get two flour deliveries a month and one root vegetable shipment from a farm in the northern district that is on its last good season. She says this the way farmers say things — without drama, because the numbers are the drama.

You have the seeds in your pack.

You brought them for the city. For the Chancellor, or whoever represents the city officially. You brought them as a gift.

Looking at this kitchen — at Mira's hands, at the careful rationing on the prep table, at the single small window that looks out on amber light and no soil — you understand that the city isn't the Chancellor.

*What do you do with the seeds?*

</details>
