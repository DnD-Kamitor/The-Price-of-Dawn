### One Sentence Pitch

A damaged ritual anchor beneath a Lowmark Dawnhall begins harming the sick people above, while Restorers and desperate locals try to keep it active because they believe it may prove that dawn can return.

### The Situation

The party arrives at a Dawnhall in Lowmark during a public disturbance.

The lanterns are burning too brightly. People upstairs are coughing and panicking. Below the hall, an old ritual anchor is pulsing with grey-amber light.

The Healers want it stopped.

The Restorers want it preserved.

The locals want hope.

The party has to act before the Dawnhall becomes a disaster site.

### 1.4 The Main Conflict

|---|---|

| Restorers | Keep the anchor active long enough to learn from it |
| Desperate locals | Protect what they think might be a chance at dawn |
| Dawnhall workers | Keep the hall safe |
| Party | Decide what matters most |

### 1.5 The Main Question

Can the party stop the danger without turning frightened people into enemies?

The party can:

- Calm the locals
- Investigate the anchor

- Destroy the anchor
- Protect civilians
- Fight if needed
- Split up and handle multiple problems at once

## 2. Dramatic Purpose

### 2.1 Why This Encounter Exists

This encounter exists to show that power in Varenhold has consequences.

The characters are strong enough to win a fight. That is not the interesting part.

The interesting part is whether they can solve the situation without making the city worse.

This is also the first real test of the gestalt system. The encounter should show what the characters can do when they are under pressure from several directions at once.

### 2.2 Dramatic Question

Can the party stop a dangerous ritual experiment without turning frightened people into enemies?

This question should guide the whole encounter.

When the players act, the GM should think about how their actions affect:

- The anchor
- The sick people above
- The Restorers
- The desperate locals
- The party’s reputation
- The future of Lowmark

### 2.3 What Should The Players Feel?

The players should feel:

- Pressure
- Doubt
- Urgency
- Sympathy
- Suspicion
- Responsibility

They should not feel that this is a simple good versus evil fight.

They should feel that every choice solves one problem and risks creating another.

### 2.4 What Should The Players Learn?

The players should learn:

- The city is not stable
- The old ritual still has consequences
- The Restorers are dangerous, but not mindless villains
- Lowmark residents are desperate for real change
- Grey sickness is not only a medical problem, but also a social and political problem
- The Dawnhalls are important because they are places where the city tries to keep itself alive
- In this campaign, violence can work, but it is rarely clean

### 2.5 GM Reminder

Do not explain all of this as lore.

Show it through the situation.

Show it through:

- Sick people coughing above
- Locals blocking the party because they are afraid
- Restorers speaking with conviction instead of madness
- The anchor getting worse while people argue
- The consequences after the party acts

The purpose of the encounter is not to tell the players what Varenhold is.

The purpose is to make them feel what Varenhold is.

### 3. The Conflict

### 3.1 The Core Conflict

Different groups want different things.

All of them have understandable reasons.

No one has the whole truth.

### 3.2 Conflict Table

| Group | What They Want | Why They Want It | What They Fear |
|---|---|---|---|
| Healers’ Guild | Stop the anchor | Patients are worsening | A public health disaster |
| Restorers | Keep the anchor active | They believe it proves dawn can return | The city giving up forever |
| Desperate locals | Help the Restorers | They are tired of waiting | Their children dying in twilight |
| Council | Keep this quiet | Public panic and political damage | Losing control of the city |
| Dawnhall workers | Protect the Dawnhall | It is a place of aid and community | The hall becoming unsafe |
| Party | Decide during play | Depends on player values | Depends on player choices |

### 3.3 The Unresolvable Tension

The anchor might contain useful information.

Keeping it active harms people.

Destroying it stops the immediate danger.

Destroying it may also erase evidence.

### 4. The Location

### 4.1 Location Purpose

The Dawnhall is not a backdrop.

It is a public place where people come for food, care, warmth, news, grief, and argument.

That makes the danger personal.

If the party fights carelessly, people suffer.

If the party hesitates too long, people suffer.

If the party handles it well, people remember.

### 4.2 Dawnhall Above

- Public hall:
  - A wide communal room with long tables, amber lanterns, notice boards, ration lists, prayer cloths, and people trying to keep daily life moving.

- Sickroom:
  - A side chamber screened off with worn cloth.
  - Several grey sickness patients are resting there.
  - The anchor pulse below makes them cough, sweat, and mutter in their sleep.

- Food distribution area:
  - A serving counter with pots of Lowmark stew, ration crates, bread baskets, and a ledger of names.
  - A worker is trying to keep people calm while the lanterns flicker too brightly.

- Memorial space:
  - A quiet wall of names, old candles, faded ribbons, and small objects left for the dead.
  - Some names are recent.

- Civilians present:
  - Dawnhall workers
  - Grey sickness patients
  - Worried family members
  - Hungry locals
  - A few children
  - One or two people who believe the Restorers may be right

- What can be heard from above:
  - Coughing
  - Panic
  - Prayers
  - Bell sounds
  - Footsteps
  - A child asking if the dawn has come
  - Someone shouting for a healer

### 4.3 Basement Below

- Mood:
  - Hot, cramped, amber-lit, unstable.
  - The room feels too small for the amount of fear inside it.

- Lighting:
  - Too bright near the lantern racks
  - Too grey near the anchor
  - No true shadows
  - Faces look tired and strange in the mixed light

- Sound:
  - Bell-metal humming
  - Pipes knocking
  - Lantern glass vibrating
  - Faint voices from above
  - The anchor pulsing like a slow, sick heartbeat

- Smell:
  - Old stone
  - Burned oil
  - Damp cloth
  - Medicine
  - Metal and dust
  - Stew from the hall above

- Immediate impression:
  - This is not a lair.
  - This is a maintenance space under a place people need.
  - That makes every spell, every shove, and every missed attack matter.

## 5. The Situation When The Party Arrives

### 5.1 What Is Happening Right Now

- The Primer Anchor is active
- The lanterns are burning too brightly
- Sick people above are getting worse
- Restorers are trying to keep the anchor stable
- Desperate locals are helping them
- Magical symptoms are beginning to manifest
- The situation has not yet become public chaos

### 5.2 What Is Really Going On

A Restorer cell found the Primer Anchor hidden beneath the Dawnhall.

They believe it is proof that the old ritual was not a complete failure.

They have connected it to the Dawnhall lantern system to make it respond.

It is responding, but not safely.

The anchor is pulling light, heat, and life through the building. The lanterns above burn brighter, but the sick grow weaker. The Restorers think this is the first painful step toward a breakthrough.

They are wrong.

The anchor is not awakening dawn.

It is creating feedback.

If it is not stopped, the Dawnhall will become the first visible disaster of something much larger.

### 5.3 What The Party Sees First

When the party reaches the basement, they see:

- Restorers working near the anchor
- Desperate locals blocking the way
- Lantern glass shaking in the racks
- Grey-amber light pulsing from the floor
- Ritual markings drawn over older maintenance stones
- Someone above coughing badly
- A strange echo forming in the corner of the room

The Restorers do not attack immediately unless the party does.

The locals are afraid, not brave.

The anchor is already dangerous.

### 5.4 First Decision Point

The party must decide what matters first.

They can:

- Talk to the Restorers
- Push past the locals
- Attack first
- Rush the anchor
- Protect the stairwell
- Investigate the device
- Evacuate the Dawnhall
- Split their attention

The correct answer is not fixed.

Each choice should change the situation.

### 5.5 GM Note

Do not start with initiative unless the players force it.

Start with tension.

Let the room breathe for a moment.

Give the Lantern-Saint one chance to speak.

Give the players one chance to choose.

Then let the anchor make waiting dangerous.

## 7. Player Approaches

This section is not a menu the players must choose from.

It is a reminder for the GM.

Let the players create their own approach, then use the closest option below to judge what happens next.

### 7.1 Talk First

Possible results:

- Delays combat
- Lowers enemy morale
- Reveals what the Restorers believe
- Creates a non-lethal path
- Buys time for another character to inspect the anchor

Risks:

- The anchor keeps escalating
- The sickroom above worsens
- The Restorers may think the party is stalling
- The locals may become more anxious

Useful GM response:

- Let Maerin speak clearly
- Let one local interrupt emotionally
- Let the anchor pulse before the conversation feels safe

### 7.2 Attack First

Possible results:

- Quickly removes opposition
- Stops ritual assistants
- Gives the party control of the room
- Tests damage, initiative, and burst power

Risks:

- Locals panic
- Restorers become martyrs
- Evidence may be destroyed
- The Dawnhall becomes a crime scene instead of a rescue
- Faction consequences worsen

Useful GM response:

- Do not punish combat automatically
- Let violence work
- Show who saw it, who ran, and who will talk about it later

### 7.3 Rush The Anchor

Possible results:

- Targets the real danger quickly
- Forces enemies to react
- Lets skill-based characters shine
- Tests movement, defense, and teamwork

Risks:

- Characters become exposed
- Anchor backlash hits the first person who touches it
- Enemies focus on stopping them
- Destroying the anchor may erase clues

Useful GM response:

- Make the anchor react immediately
- Let defenders move to block access
- Reward smart skill use over simple damage

### 7.4 Protect Civilians

Possible results:

- Lowers casualties
- Improves reputation
- Gives social leverage over locals
- Earns Healers’ Guild approval
- Shows what kind of heroes the party are

Risks:

- Gives the Restorers more time
- Splits the party
- Leaves the anchor active longer
- May make the main fight harder

Useful GM response:

- Let saved civilians matter
- Let locals hesitate when they see the party protecting people
- Keep the anchor clock moving

### 7.5 Investigate Before Acting

Possible results:

- Gives better information
- Makes stabilization easier
- Reveals a hidden clue
- Creates stronger arguments in social conflict
- Lets intelligent and observant characters shine

Risks:

- Costs time
- Does not stop immediate escalation
- Requires protection from other PCs
- May look like hesitation to frightened locals

Useful GM response:

- Give useful information quickly
- Do not hide everything behind one roll
- Reveal both opportunity and danger

### 7.6 Split The Party

Possible results:

- Multiple problems are handled at once
- Lets different characters shine
- Tests the gestalt system properly
- Creates a dynamic scene

Risks:

- Isolated characters can be pressured
- Healing and support may be harder
- Mistakes become more costly
- The GM must track more moving parts

Useful GM response:

- Cut between groups often
- Keep each group under pressure
- Let success in one area help another area

### 7.7 GM Reminder

Do not decide the correct solution in advance.

Each approach should work, but each approach should cost something.

That is the encounter.

## 8. Encounter Mechanics

### 8.1 The Anchor

The anchor is the central pressure point.

It can be:

- Stabilized
- Damaged
- Destroyed
- Misused
- Ignored

It should always feel dangerous, but not impossible to understand.

Use simple rules.

The anchor acts on initiative count 20.

### 8.2 Anchor Statistics

| Stat | Value |
|---|---:|
| AC | 15 |
| HP | 40 |
| Damage resistance | Radiant, fire |
| Damage vulnerability | Thunder |
| Damage immunity | Poison, psychic |
| Condition immunity | All conditions |

The anchor is an object, not a creature.

It does not move.

It does not think.

It reacts.

### 8.3 Stabilizing The Anchor

Stabilizing the anchor is the cleanest solution.

Required successes:

- 3 successes

Allowed failures:

- 2 failures

On the third failure, the anchor flares.

Each attempt takes an action.

Default DC:

- DC 14

Increase to DC 16 if:

- The character is under attack
- The anchor has been damaged
- An Anchor-Bearer is actively interfering
- The character is using the wrong tool for the problem

Suggested skills:

| Skill | Use |
|---|---|
| Arcana | Understand magical feedback |
| Religion | Understand ritual or divine logic |
| Investigation | Find physical calibration marks |
| Sleight of Hand | Adjust delicate lantern lenses |
| Athletics | Force the anchor ring out of alignment |
| Medicine | Read patient symptoms through the pulse rhythm |

On a success:

- Mark 1 success
- The anchor pulse weakens for a moment
- The sickroom above gets slightly quieter

On a failure:

- Mark 1 failure
- The acting character takes 1d6 radiant damage
- The lanterns above flare brighter

On 3 successes:

- The anchor stabilizes
- Light-Sick Echoes weaken or vanish
- The sick people above stop worsening
- The Restorers lose their immediate reason to fight

On 3 failures:

- The anchor flares
- Trigger the final flare effect from the round escalation chapter

### 8.4 Helping With The Anchor

A character can help another character if they can explain how they help.

Examples:

- Holding the anchor ring steady
- Reading markings aloud
- Keeping lantern lenses aligned
- Shielding the acting character
- Calling out changes in the sickroom above

Helping gives advantage on the check.

Only one character can help per check.

### 8.5 Damaging The Anchor

The anchor can be attacked.

This is simple and direct, but risky.

When the anchor takes damage for the first time:

- The room shakes
- The lanterns scream like metal on glass
- Everyone understands the party may be destroying evidence

When the anchor takes 15 or more damage in one round:

- It releases a backlash
- Creatures within 10 feet make a DC 13 Constitution save
- On a failure, they take 2d6 radiant damage
- On a success, they take half damage

Damaging the anchor does not count as stabilizing it.

### 8.6 Destroying The Anchor

When the anchor reaches 0 HP, it is destroyed.

Immediate result:

- The pulse stops
- The Light-Sick Echoes vanish
- The sick people above stop worsening
- The immediate danger ends

Long-term consequence:

- Some evidence is lost
- The Restorers are furious
- The Council may want the remains controlled
- The Healers may be relieved, but frustrated by lost information

Evidence lost:

- The anchor’s active pattern
- Its current connection to the lantern system
- Part of its link to the old ritual

Evidence that remains:

- Broken fragments
- Burn marks
- Ritual markings
- Witness testimony
- The fact that the anchor reacted at all

### 8.7 Misusing The Anchor

A character may try to use the anchor instead of stopping it.

Examples:

- Draw power from it
- Redirect its pulse
- Force it to reveal a vision
- Use it against enemies
- Connect it more deeply to the lanterns

This should be possible, but dangerous.

Default check:

- DC 16 Arcana or Religion

On success:

- The character gets one useful effect
- The anchor also escalates

Possible useful effects:

- Reveal a vision of the old ritual
- Stun one Light-Sick Echo for 1 round
- Give advantage on the next stabilization check
- Force Maerin to realize the anchor is unstable

On failure:

- The character takes 2d6 radiant damage
- The anchor immediately triggers its next escalation effect

GM reminder:

Let clever ideas work.

But the anchor is not safe.

### 8.8 Ignoring The Anchor

If the party ignores the anchor, it escalates every round.

This is not punishment.

This is the situation continuing.

The anchor should make waiting feel costly.

Use the round escalation table in Chapter 9.

### 8.9 Simple Rule For The GM

If the players interact with the anchor, ask:

- Are they trying to understand it?
- Are they trying to stabilize it?
- Are they trying to break it?
- Are they trying to use it?

Then choose the closest rule.

Do not stop the game to find the perfect mechanic.


## 9. Round Escalation

### 9.1 Initiative Count 20

At initiative count 20, the anchor acts.

If the anchor is stabilized or destroyed, stop using this table.

| Round | Anchor Effect |
|---|---|
| 1 | The anchor pulses. Lantern glass rings. Everyone feels pressure behind the eyes. No damage yet. |
| 2 | Creatures within 10 feet of the anchor make a DC 13 Constitution save or take 2d6 radiant damage. Half damage on success. |
| 3 | The sickroom above worsens. A healer shouts down for help. Social checks against locals become harder if no one is helping civilians. |
| 4 | One Light-Sick Echo appears near a lantern rack or broken pipe. |
| 5 | The anchor pulls at memory and grief. Each PC hears a voice from above asking for dawn. DC 13 Wisdom save or disadvantage on their next attack roll or ability check. |
| 6 | Final flare. All creatures in the basement make a DC 14 Constitution save. Failure: 4d6 radiant damage and blinded until the end of their next turn. Success: half damage and not blinded. |

### 9.2 Escalation Triggers

Use these when appropriate.

Do not use every trigger every time.

Pick the ones that make the scene sharper.

| Trigger | Effect |
|---|---|
| First blood | The locals panic. One desperate local tries to flee upstairs unless stopped or calmed. |
| First death | Maerin either calls for everyone to stop, or becomes fully committed. Choose based on who died and how. |
| Anchor damaged | The anchor screams with light. Creatures within 10 feet make a DC 13 Constitution save or take 2d6 radiant damage. |
| Anchor stabilized | The echoes weaken. Restorers lose morale. Locals become easier to calm. |
| Civilian endangered | Dawnhall workers turn against whoever caused the danger. Locals may stop fighting to help. |
| Party uses radiant magic | The anchor flares in response. The next anchor DC increases by 1. |
| Party invokes a god | Maerin listens for one moment. The next social check against them has advantage if the appeal is sincere. |
| Party mentions Corven | Maerin becomes suspicious and demands to know what the party knows. |
| Party mentions the Night of the Ritual | The room goes quiet for one breath. Then the anchor pulses harder. Advance the escalation by 1 round. |

### 9.3 GM Reminder

The escalation is there to keep the scene moving.

It should create urgency, not remove choice.

If the players are acting quickly, reward that.

If they are arguing too long, let the anchor remind them that the world does not wait.

| Anchor-Bearers | No | Yes, if leader falls | Yes, the Lantern-Saint |
| Desperate locals | No | Yes | Yes, their hope |
| Light-Sick Echoes | Instinctively hostile | No | No |

### 10.3 How To Run Them

#### Restorer Lantern-Saint

Use them to keep the conflict human.

They should:

- Speak before fighting
- Challenge the party’s motives
- Protect the anchor
- Order others to avoid killing
- Become more dangerous if the party uses brutal force

They should not:

- Act like a mad cultist
- Fight to the death for no reason
- Ignore civilian suffering once it is obvious

#### Restorer Anchor-Bearers

Use them to block, shove, pull, and protect.

They should:

- Stand between the party and the anchor
- Protect the Lantern-Saint
- Use non-lethal force first
- Focus on delaying the party

They should not:

- Chase weak characters away from the main scene
- Fight after the Lantern-Saint stands down
- Keep fighting if the anchor clearly becomes a disaster

#### Dawnhall Desperate

Use them as moral pressure.

They should:

- Block paths
- Shout warnings
- Panic when blood is spilled
- Help the Restorers until they see the cost

They should not:

- Fight like trained soldiers
- Die pointlessly
- Stay hostile if the party protects civilians

#### Light-Sick Echoes

Use them as proof that the anchor is unstable.

They should:

- Attack the nearest living creature
- Crawl from lantern racks, pipes, or cracks
- Make the room feel unsafe
- Force the party to split attention

They should not:

- Speak
- Negotiate
- Retreat
- Have complex tactics

### 10.4 Morale

Use morale to keep the scene alive.

| Event | Enemy Reaction |
|---|---|
| Anchor stabilized | Restorers hesitate or stop |
| Lantern-Saint falls | Anchor-Bearers try to retreat or surrender |
| Two desperate locals fall | Remaining locals panic |
| Civilian is harmed | Everyone reacts, even enemies |
| Light-Sick Echoes multiply | Restorers begin to doubt the plan |
| Party protects civilians | Locals become easier to calm |

### 10.5 GM Reminder

Do not run every opponent as a monster.

Run them as people under pressure.

Only the Light-Sick Echoes are pure danger.


## 11. Social Conflict

### 11.1 Talking During Combat

Talking is allowed during combat.

A short sentence is free.

Free sentence:

- Warning
- Plea
- Insult
- Question
- Command
- Quick truth

Requires an action:

- Serious persuasion
- Intimidation
- Detailed explanation
- Calming a local
- Convincing someone to stand down
- Making a clear offer

Default DC:

- DC 13 if the argument is strong
- DC 15 if the target is afraid or angry
- DC 17 if blood has already been spilled

### 11.2 Arguments That Can Work

| Argument | Target | Possible Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Protect the sick above | Locals, Lantern-Saint | Reduces hostility |
| Preserve the evidence | Lantern-Saint | Opens negotiation |
| This is harming Lowmark | Locals | Makes them hesitate |
| You are being used | Anchor-Bearers | Creates doubt |
| We can stop this without killing | Everyone | Opens non-lethal path |
| Let us stabilize it, not destroy it | Lantern-Saint | Buys one round |
| Look at what the anchor is doing | Everyone | Forces doubt after an anchor pulse |

### 11.3 Arguments That Should Not Work Easily

These arguments should either fail or increase tension unless supported by strong evidence.

- “The Council knows best”
- “You are all criminals”
- “Stand down or die”
- “The city must wait”
- “Your hope is stupid”
- “Trust us because we say so”

These people are desperate, not foolish.

Disrespect should make them harder to reach.

### 11.4 Ending Without Killing

The fight can end without killing if enough pressure is removed.

Possible conditions:

- Anchor is stabilized
- Lantern-Saint is convinced
- Desperate locals are calmed
- At least one PC protects civilians
- The party offers a believable next step
- The Restorers are allowed to surrender without humiliation

Possible next steps:

- Preserve anchor fragments for study
- Let the Healers inspect the effect on patients
- Promise Maerin that the evidence will not vanish into Council hands
- Escort the locals upstairs safely
- Let the Restorers leave under watch instead of executing them

### 11.5 Social Turning Points

Use these when the party makes a good argument or takes a meaningful action.

| Event | Effect |
|---|---|
| PC protects a civilian | Locals become easier to calm |
| PC heals a sick person | Healers support the party openly |
| PC stabilizes one part of the anchor | Maerin becomes willing to listen |
| PC destroys evidence recklessly | Restorers become harder to calm |
| PC kills a local | Social resolution almost collapses |
| PC offers a real compromise | Lantern-Saint may pause the fight |

### 11.6 GM Reminder

Do not stop the danger while people talk.

The anchor keeps pulsing.

That is what makes the conversation matter.

A good speech should not solve everything.

A good speech should change what someone does next.

## 12. Clues And Information

Do not hide essential information behind a roll.

Use checks to give better, faster, or more specific information.

### 12.1 Before The Encounter

| Check | Information |
|---|---|
| Investigation | The basement door was opened recently. The lock was not forced. Someone had access. |
| Insight | The locals are frightened, not aggressive by nature. They expect to be ignored or pushed aside. |
| Religion | The markings are not ordinary prayer marks. They are tied to restoration, dawn, and ritual memory. |
| Arcana | The lanterns are reacting to something below. This is feedback, not a stable ritual. |
| Medicine | The grey sickness patients worsen whenever the lanterns flare. The pulse is affecting them directly. |
| History | Old civic buildings sometimes hide sealed infrastructure from before or shortly after the Night of the Ritual. |

### 12.2 During The Encounter

| Check | Information |
|---|---|
| Perception | The next Light-Sick Echo is forming near the most unstable lantern rack. |
| Arcana | The anchor can be stabilized. Destroying it is not the only option. |
| Religion | The ritual logic is incomplete. The Restorers are working from fragments. |
| Medicine | The sickroom above is close to crisis. If the pulse continues, someone may die. |
| Persuasion | Maerin can be reached through protecting the living, not through defending the Council. |
| Intimidation | Threats can scatter locals, but they make the Restorers harder to calm. |

### 12.3 After The Encounter

| Check | Information |
|---|---|
| Investigation | A ledger entry shows someone accessed the basement records recently. |
| Arcana | The anchor was not creating dawn. It was amplifying unstable ritual residue. |
| History | This was likely a minor ritual component, not the heart of the old working. |
| Insight | Maerin believed what they were saying, but someone may have given them the method. |

## 13. Consequences

Consequences should create future play.

Do not use them only as punishment.

### 13.1 Clean Success

Conditions:

- Anchor stabilized
- Civilians protected
- Evidence preserved
- No unnecessary deaths

Consequences:

- The Healers’ Guild trusts the party
- Lowmark hears that the party protected people
- Maerin may become a future contact instead of an enemy
- The party gains useful evidence about the old ritual
- The Council wants a private report

### 13.2 Practical Success

Conditions:

- Anchor stopped
- Some harm caused
- Some evidence lost
- Violence happened, but not excessively

Consequences:

- The Dawnhall survives, but people are shaken
- The Healers are grateful, but frustrated
- The Restorers blame the party for ruining a chance
- Lowmark opinion is divided
- The party gets partial information

### 13.3 Brutal Success

Conditions:

- Anchor stopped
- Restorers killed
- Locals harmed or terrified
- Public scandal begins

Consequences:

- The immediate danger ends
- Lowmark fears the party
- The Restorers gain martyrs
- The Council may publicly distance itself from the party
- Future social encounters in Lowmark become harder

### 13.4 Failure

Conditions:

- Anchor flares
- Civilians harmed
- Evidence destroyed
- Factions blame the party or each other

Consequences:

- The Dawnhall becomes unsafe
- Grey sickness cases worsen nearby
- The Healers are angry or devastated
- The Council tries to control the story
- The Restorers claim the party destroyed hope
- The party starts Session 1 with a damaged reputation

### 13.5 Secret Success

Conditions:

- Hidden clue found
- Someone important spared
- Evidence quietly recovered
- Party notices a deeper pattern

Consequences:

- The party learns the Restorers did not act alone
- The anchor points to another sealed site
- Maerin may secretly contact the party later
- The Healers gain a lead on grey sickness
- The campaign gains a new investigation thread

## 14. Faction Reactions

### 14.1 Healers’ Guild

- Clean success:
  - The Healers trust the party.
  - They offer medical information and quiet support.

- Violent success:
  - The Healers are relieved the danger ended, but disturbed by the cost.
  - They become cautious with the party.

- Failure:
  - The Healers blame the party for not acting fast enough.
  - They may still work with the party, but without warmth.

### 14.2 Council

- Clean success:
  - The Council wants a private report.
  - They prefer the matter to stay quiet.

- Violent success:
  - The Council may use the party as useful deniable force.
  - Publicly, they may distance themselves.

- Failure:
  - The Council looks for someone to blame.
  - The party becomes politically inconvenient.

### 14.3 Restorers

- Clean success:
  - Some Restorers respect the party for preserving evidence.
  - Others believe the party interfered with hope.

- Violent success:
  - The Restorers gain martyrs.
  - Future Restorer encounters become more hostile.

- Failure:
  - The Restorers twist the event into proof that the city fears restoration.
  - Maerin or another Restorer may become more radical.

### 14.4 Lowmark Residents

- Clean success:
  - Lowmark hears that the party protected people.
  - The party gains cautious goodwill.

- Violent success:
  - Lowmark becomes divided.
  - Some are grateful, others afraid.

- Failure:
  - Lowmark remembers the Dawnhall disaster.
  - The party is seen as another group that failed them.

### 14.5 The Spire

- Clean success:
  - The Spire becomes interested in the preserved evidence.
  - They may request access to the anchor fragments.

- Violent success:
  - The Spire is frustrated by lost information.
  - They may still study what remains.

- Failure:
  - The Spire treats the event as a dangerous uncontrolled breach.
  - They may push for stricter control over old ritual sites.

### 16.4 6 Gestalt Characters

- Add:
  - 1 Restorer Anchor-Bearer
  - 2 Dawnhall Desperate
  - 1 Light-Sick Echo

- Increase:
  - Anchor HP from 40 to 55
  - Final flare DC from 14 to 15

- Add second pressure point:
  - A lantern regulator near the stairwell begins overheating
  - It can be cooled or disabled with DC 14 Arcana, Investigation, Sleight of Hand, or Athletics
  - If ignored for 2 rounds, it bursts and threatens civilians above

### 16.5 If The Party Is Very Optimized

Do not just add hit points.

Add pressure.

Good options:

- Start one Light-Sick Echo already active
- Place an Anchor-Bearer between the party and the anchor
- Make the anchor pulse affect concentration
- Add civilians near the stairwell
- Make the ledger clue vulnerable to fire, thunder, or reckless attacks
- Have Maerin order a retreat with evidence if the party dominates too quickly

Use complications that create choices.

Do not turn the encounter into a slog.

### 16.6 If The Party Is Struggling

Ease the pressure without making it obvious.

Good options:

- Delay the Round 4 Light-Sick Echo
- Let Maerin call for restraint
- Let a desperate local change sides after seeing the anchor hurt someone
- Reduce anchor pulse damage by 1d6
- Let one successful social action remove a desperate local from the fight
- Let Ilya Ren shout useful information from above
- Allow partial success if the party stabilizes part of the anchor

The goal is not to crush the party.

The goal is to learn what their characters can handle.

## 17. Read-Aloud Text

### 17.1 Arrival At The Dawnhall

The Dawnhall is louder than it should be.

Amber lanterns burn above the entrance, too bright in the grey half-light of Lowmark. People crowd the doorway. Some are arguing, some are praying, and some are simply standing still with the tired look of people who have run out of better choices.

From inside, you hear coughing.

Then a bell rings once from somewhere within the hall.

Not a warning yet.

A plea.

### 17.2 Entering The Basement

The stairs down are narrow and warm.

At the bottom, the basement opens into a cramped chamber of stone, pipes, storage shelves, and trembling lantern racks. In the center of the room, something old is awake.

A ring of stone and metal pulses with grey-amber light.

Restorers stand around it, hands raised, voices low. A few Lowmark locals block the path behind them. They look frightened, not brave.

The lantern glass rattles.

Above you, someone coughs hard enough to choke.

### 17.3 First Anchor Pulse

The anchor pulses.

For one heartbeat, every lantern in the basement burns white-hot. The air presses against your skin. Dust falls from the ceiling. Somewhere above, a healer shouts for help.

Then the light drops back to amber.

The room is quiet for half a breath.

Then everyone starts moving.

### 17.4 Lantern-Saint Speech

The Restorer at the anchor turns toward you.

Their face is calm, but their hands are shaking.

“Do not touch it.”

They glance up, toward the sickroom above.

“I know what it is doing. I know what it costs. But this is the first answer this city has given us in fifty years.”

The anchor pulses behind them.

“If you came only to destroy it, then you came too late.”

### 17.5 Panic Above

A crash echoes from the hall above.

Someone screams. Another voice calls for water. The floorboards overhead shake with running feet.

Then you hear a child’s voice, small and terrified.

“Is it dawn?”

The question hangs in the basement longer than it should.

### 17.6 Anchor Stabilized

The anchor gives one final pulse.

Then the light folds inward.

The lanterns stop screaming. The pipes stop knocking. The sick pressure in the air breaks like a fever.

Above you, the coughing does not vanish, but it eases.

For the first time since you entered, the basement feels like a room again.

Maerin stares at the anchor in silence.

No one cheers.

### 17.7 Anchor Destroyed

The anchor cracks.

Grey-amber light spills through the fractures, too bright to look at, then collapses into smoke and dust.

The lanterns above flare once and go dim.

The echoes vanish.

The danger is over.

But the chamber is full of broken stone, burned markings, and people staring at what cannot be studied anymore.

Maerin whispers, “You have no idea what you just buried.”

### 17.8 Aftermath

When you return upstairs, the Dawnhall is still standing.

That feels like a victory.

But the room is changed.

Patients breathe under damp cloths. Workers sweep broken bowls from the floor. Locals whisper in corners, watching you with fear, gratitude, anger, or all three.

Outside, Lowmark waits in the grey-amber light.

Someone will tell this story by morning.

If morning ever comes.

## 18. DM Quick Reference

### 18.1 What Matters Most

- The anchor is the real danger
- The Restorers are not cartoon villains
- The locals are desperate, not stupid
- The Dawnhall is public and symbolic
- Violence works, but it creates consequences
- The best outcome requires restraint and action

### 18.2 Things To Say Often

Use these as short reminders during play:

- The light is too bright
- There are no proper shadows
- Someone is coughing upstairs
- The lantern glass is vibrating
- The locals look afraid, not evil
- The anchor pulse feels almost like a heartbeat
- The room is getting hotter
- The Dawnhall above is not empty
- Every delay has a sound

### 18.3 Things Not To Do

- Do not make this a simple monster fight
- Do not force one correct solution
- Do not make social play pause the danger
- Do not make every enemy fight to the death
- Do not remove consequences from reckless actions
- Do not punish clever plans
- Do not explain too much lore during combat
- Do not make the locals foolish
- Do not make the Restorers randomly cruel

### 18.4 Improvisation Rule

When the players do something unexpected, ask:

1. Does it protect people?
2. Does it control the anchor?
3. Does it reveal truth?
4. Does it change someone’s mind?
5. Does it create a cost?

Then respond honestly from the world.

### 18.5 If You Get Stuck

Pick one thing to move the scene forward:

- The anchor pulses
- Someone upstairs screams
- Maerin makes a demand
- A local panics
- A Light-Sick Echo appears
- A lantern rack starts to crack
- A healer shouts new information
- An Anchor-Bearer moves to block the party

### 18.6 Fast DCs

Use these if you need a quick ruling:

| Situation | DC |
|---|---:|
| Easy but tense | 12 |
| Standard pressure | 14 |
| Hard under danger | 16 |
| Very hard or risky | 18 |

### 18.7 Fast Consequences

| Player Action | Likely Consequence |
|---|---|
| Protects civilians | Locals become easier to calm |
| Attacks first | Restorers become harder to negotiate with |
| Damages the anchor | Immediate backlash or lost evidence |
| Stabilizes part of the anchor | Maerin hesitates |
| Kills a local | Social resolution almost collapses |
| Preserves evidence | Future faction options stay open |
| Ignores the anchor | Escalation continues |
| Offers a real compromise | The fight can pause |

### 18.8 Final Reminder

Run the situation, not the script.

The anchor wants to flare.

The Healers want people saved.

The Restorers want proof.

The locals want hope.

The Dawnhall wants to survive the day.

Let the players step into that pressure and see what they do.
