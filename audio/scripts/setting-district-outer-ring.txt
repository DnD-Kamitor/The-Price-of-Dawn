The Outer Ring was designed to impress visiting dignitaries and it still tries, even as the lacquer cracks. Broad avenues sweep past guild halls with shuttered upper floors, the Council Hall keeps its lanterns lit out of stubbornness, and the Auris Cathedral stands physically split between Penitent mourning cloth and Wounded defiance. The scent is beeswax and expensive perfume instead of lamp oil. Conversations are negotiations, even when they sound like greetings.

Here you watch power pretend nothing has changed. Chancellor Ostenveld's lamp burns through the night while couriers jog between buildings with seals that have already been broken and resealed. Saret Onn counts crates in the street because she trusts her own ledger more than the Compact's promises. Cathedral choirs duel across a temporary wall that has been there twelve years, each hymn trying to claim Auris's favor. Restorer pamphlets appear on marble doors before guards can decide whether removing them is treason. Everyone maintains the fiction of stability because that is what the Outer Ring sells to the rest of the city. When the fiction falters, you can feel the whole arc tighten like a bowstring.
