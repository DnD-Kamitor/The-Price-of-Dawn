The Lowmark sits against the river, damp air rolling through streets that always smell like Lowmark Stew—preserved vegetables, marsh oil, something warm enough to keep you upright. Buildings here lean over narrow lanes with washing lines strung between windows, and every surface carries the sheen of river mist. You hear coughing before you turn the first corner. Dockworkers argue over manifests, children run between market stalls, and somewhere in the background a Dawnhall cook counts portions under her breath.

This is where the city keeps itself alive. The Lowmark Dawnhall serves breakfast twice a day, the Healing House reeks of dried herbs and faint sweetness from Lira Anwick's compounds, and Wess at the Grain Measure guards ledgers that are more frightening than any monster. Queues form quietly, anger curdles into resolve, and people who cannot afford to slow down simply stand still until a neighbor presses a hand to their shoulder. Watch for Orya Doss carrying more supplies than should fit in her pack, for Desperate pamphlets that do not lie, and for grey sickness patients trying to pretend they are only tired. The Lowmark knows exactly how close Varenhold is to collapse, and it keeps stirring the stew anyway.
