The Ashfen Gate district is the city's inhale and exhale. Southward winds carry marsh-cold air through gatehouses built wide for caravans, bringing the smell of wet vegetation, animal sweat, and Ashfen oil before you even see the walls. Streets here are practical—inns, stables, customs offices, the Restorer compound occupying an old trading post with its shutters permanently open to the public hall.

Travelers arrive coated in road dust and leave with Dawnhall bread in their packs. Helka at the Wayfarer's Rest keeps a guest book that doubles as the city's memory; she can tell you which partner cities have stopped sending caravans because she has twenty years of signatures. Restorer services spill into courtyards when grief outgrows the pews. Guards at the gate argue theology with Penitent observers while trying to keep paperwork honest. Outside the walls, the Wanderer's Wayshrine gathers offerings from people whose journeys depend on more than lantern light. When a caravan departs and the gate stays closed, everyone in this district counts the hours until the hinges move again.
