The Dawnhalls district smells like dinner even at dawn. Warm broth, woodsmoke, lamp oil, the reassurance of bread ovens that never go dark—the air alone tells you this is the part of Varenhold that refuses to surrender. Buildings here are new by local standards: heavy timber, covered walkways connecting kitchens, dormitories, workshops. The Great Dawnhall glows like a beacon, its memorial room tended by Nessa with the same care she once gave the living.

This is where the Dawnborn sleep and where the city comes to remember it is a community. Sera walks the streets in civilian clothes taking the temperature of her people. Tomas folds legal complaints at a ground-floor table that doubles as a counseling bench. Lira slips through on her way to the Healing Houses, tired and unstoppable. Children play in the courtyard inventing rules about who gets to be Sera, Tomas, or Lira in the game. Lantern Workshop crews swap shifts to the rhythm of a work song older than the twilight. Supplies are posted on the wall like prayers, and handwritten notes remind everyone that thirty-seven percent rations is not fine. If Varenhold survives, it will be because the Dawnhalls kept cooking.
