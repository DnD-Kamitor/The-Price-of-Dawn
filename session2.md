# Session 2 — The Weight of Light (Level 5)

*Dilemma: Do you have the right to decide someone else's fate, even to save thousands?*

---

## Scene 1 — Opening Combat: The Reckoning at the Archive

**Five senses:**
- Sight: the Archive's ground floor reading room, three document bundles spread across the central table, Reckoning soldiers moving between the shelves with deliberate efficiency
- Sound: the scrape of folios being pulled, a soldier's clipped order, the thud of a bundle hitting the floor
- Smell: old paper and leather bindings, then the first thin thread of smoke from the fireplace being stoked
- Touch: the door handle still warm from whoever entered minutes ago, the draft from an open window on the west wall
- Taste: woodsmoke catching the back of the throat as you step inside

**Setup:** The players return to the Archive to follow up on Corven's documents. They arrive to find Reckoning soldiers already inside. The Reckoning's goal is specific: destroy the ritual documents before investigators can use them. Three document bundles sit on the reading tables — the Reckoning intends to burn all three. The fireplace at the room's east end is already lit and stoked. Players can see the bundles clearly before the first initiative roll.

**Secondary Objective:** Three ritual document bundles are on the reading tables, visible from the door. Each bundle takes one full action to move to the fireplace; a bundle thrown into the fire is unrecoverable. Players must keep at least two bundles out of the fire. The secondary objective is visible and active from the moment the scene opens — soldiers are already reaching for the nearest bundle on round 1.

**Enemies:**
- Reckoning Soldiers: 4, use Veteran stats (MM p.350), here to burn documents not to hold ground — they prioritise the bundles over fighting players
- Reckoning Lieutenant: 1, use Knight stats (MM p.347), coordinating the burn — the only one who might have information about further Reckoning plans if captured

**Combat ends when:** The Lieutenant is captured or incapacitated, or when two bundles are secured in players' hands or moved away from the fireplace. At that point the soldiers withdraw in good order — they were here for a task, not a battle.

**What players learn from this scene:** The Reckoning knows what the documents say. They are not trying to understand the ritual. They are trying to prevent others from understanding it in time to do something other than what the Reckoning intends.

## Scene 2 — The Riddle: Theron's Encoded Message

**Setup:** After the combat, Theron emerges from behind his reading alcove — he barricaded himself in when the soldiers arrived. He is shaking, but functional. He unlocks his personal desk drawer and removes three parchment slips. He sets them on the table in front of the players without speaking first. He has been carrying these for eleven years. He encoded his own knowledge so he could deny it existed. He used a simple system. He left it solvable.

**The Riddle:**
> First slip: *"The Dawnmark rises when the anchor holds. The vessel must be willing. The energy of the third circuit does not disperse; it dwells."*
>
> Second slip: *"Ask the archivist what lives within the amber light. The answer is not in the records. Within them is the truth of the ritual's end."*
>
> Third slip: *"Every third scholar agreed: the Dawnborn are the mechanism. The energy lives in willing vessels. The ritual them waits still."*

Players must identify the pattern: reading every third word of all three slips in sequence.

**Answer:** THE DAWNBORN ARE THE VESSEL THE ENERGY LIVES WITHIN THEM

**Correct:** Theron looks at the players when they read it aloud. He does not deny it. He sits down — not behind his desk but in the chair across from them, like a patient waiting for a verdict. He says: *"Yes."*

**Wrong (first attempt):** Theron watches them work. He says: *"Keep trying. The pattern is simpler than it looks."* He will not give the method, but he will confirm when they have the right approach. He has been waiting for someone to crack this. He is not going to make it harder.

**Wrong (third attempt):** Theron reaches for the slips. *"Perhaps it's better you don't know yet."* This is the moment — players must choose whether to let him take them back. If they hold onto the slips, he stops reaching. He sits down. *"All right,"* he says. *"All right."* The RP scene begins early.

**GM Note:** Theron encoded this message for himself, not against the players. The real riddle is why someone would write down a secret in a form designed to be found.

## Scene 3 — The Puzzle: The Resonance Box

**Setup:** Theron's private study, upper Archive floor. A sealed wooden box sits on the shelf behind his desk — it has been there for eleven years. Theron's physical proof of what the Dawnborn are: Corven's original ritual redirection notes, in his handwriting. The box is sealed with a resonance lock: three circular indentations on the lid, each requiring a specific name spoken aloud while the indentation is pressed. Wrong input triggers a tone and reseals the mechanism for thirty seconds.

**The Puzzle:** The three names are those of the scholars who witnessed the ritual's redirection. Players find the names in the documents from Scene 1 — they appear in the colophons of two of the three bundles, as witnesses to Corven's final notation. The names are CORVEN, SALI, WREN. Each indentation corresponds to one name. The order is left-to-right, matching the signature order on the colophon. Players who examine the recovered bundles find the names without a roll; connecting them to the box requires a DC 10 Arcana check or simply trying the names they found.

**Solution:**
1. Press the left indentation and speak CORVEN.
2. Press the centre indentation and speak SALI.
3. Press the right indentation and speak WREN.
The lid opens.

**Partial success:** If players have only one or two of the names (if a bundle was burned in Scene 1), they can attempt the known names. Getting two correct and one wrong causes the wrong-name tone but does not reset the two correct ones — the box holds partial state. They need the third name. Investigation DC 14 of the surviving bundles, or asking Theron directly (he knows the names; he has just never spoken them to the box).

**Failure:** If players attempt to open the box by force — thieves' tools DC 20 — the box opens but the seal fractures. The documents inside are readable but visibly damaged, edges scorched by the broken mechanism. Theron's expression when he sees them is not anger. It is the look of someone watching the last physical evidence of their guilt get hurt.

**Success:** Inside: Corven's original ritual redirection notes in his handwriting. The key passage, in the third page: *"The anchors are not lost. The light did not die. It redistributed into ten living vessels born at the moment of inversion. The ritual is incomplete, not failed. The completion requires their release."* Also present: Fragment 2 of the Notation Key.

**Connected to:** Fragment 2 combined with Fragment 1 from Session 1 allows partial decoding of Corven's journal notation. The third fragment exists in Session 3. Players who have both fragments enter that session with a significant advantage on the ritual diagram puzzle.

## Scene 4 — The RP Scene: Theron's Confession

**NPC:** Theron Waide
**Want:** To be heard. To stop carrying this alone. To be forgiven, though he will not ask for it directly.
**Fear:** That telling them will trigger the very thing he has spent eleven years preventing — a forced ritual, the Dawnborn treated as a mechanism rather than people.
**Lie:** That he has not decided anything. He has. He has been waiting for someone with enough courage — or enough ignorance — to investigate seriously. He chose to wait for them rather than act. He has decided this was noble. He knows it was cowardice.

**Setup:** The box is open or the riddle is decoded — either works as the trigger. Theron does not stand behind his desk. He comes around to the front of it and sits in the plain chair across from the players. The study is quiet. The document bundles are on the desk behind him. The box is on the table between them.

**Five senses:**
- Sight: Theron in the plain chair, coat still on, hands in his lap, looking at neither the box nor the documents but at the players
- Sound: the Archive settling around them, a distant creak of shelves, nothing else
- Smell: old paper and the faint char of the resonance lock's broken mechanism if it was forced open, or simply dust if it opened cleanly

**The Encounter Beat:** Theron says: *"I found it eleven years ago. I decoded the notation. I found the midwife records. I understood what Corven wrote."* He pauses. *"I told no one because once I said it, someone would have to decide something. And I didn't want to be the one who made that happen."* He looks at them. *"I've been hoping someone would come who could do what I couldn't. So I waited. I arranged the fragments where they could be found. I left the box where it could be seen."* Another pause. *"I'm very sorry for what I'm about to tell you."*

Then he tells them. The Dawnborn are the ritual. The ten people living in this city, running the food hall and tending the sick and teaching children and keeping the peace — they are Lux Anchors. They have been holding fifty years of redirected solar energy in their bodies since the night they were born. Completing the ritual means releasing that energy simultaneously. The technical term in Corven's notation is *extinguished*.

After he finishes, ask the table directly — out loud, not rhetorically: *"What does your character do with this information in the next five minutes?"*

**Sample dialogue (GM prompt, not script):**
- "I've known for eleven years. I've been counting the days. Day 4,017 was when I stopped counting — that was when I realised it didn't matter how long I waited. Nothing was going to make this easier to say."
- "You want to know why I didn't go to the Council? I tried. Once. Before Ostenveld. The response was two weeks in a holding room and my documents returned damaged. The city did not want to know."
- "I don't think I'm a coward. I think I told myself I was being careful. The difference feels smaller every year."

**How it connects forward:** The players now know the truth. They also know that Theron has been sitting on it for eleven years, that the previous Chancellor suppressed it, and that someone in the current administration may know more than they have admitted. Every conversation with a Dawnborn from this point forward is different.

## Scene 5 — Closing Combat: Harran's Arrival

**Setup:** The players are leaving the Archive with the documents. The alley behind the Archive building, or the street outside — Harran Lecht is already there. He is not here to fight. He is here to see what the players do. Three Reckoning soldiers are also present, and they do not have his restraint. Harran stands apart from them, coat open, no weapon drawn, watching.

**Secondary Objective:** Harran is trying to read what the players are carrying. He can see the document bundles. If he gets a clear look at the notation on the outer pages — DC 14 Perception on his part, which he will attempt if players don't conceal the documents — he knows enough to confirm his suspicions about what they found. The secondary objective is keeping the document contents out of his sight while handling the soldiers. This means managing two things at once: the soldiers attacking, and Harran circling for a better angle.

**Enemies:**
- Reckoning Soldiers: 3, Veteran stats (MM p.350), acting without Harran's restraint — they will fight to stop the players leaving with the documents
- Harran Lecht: 1, use Bandit Captain stats (MM p.344) with 2 additional attacks on his multiattack (4 total), but Harran does NOT attack unless attacked first — he watches, repositions, and waits

**Combat ends when:** Players hold their position for 3 consecutive rounds without retreating, or Harran chooses to leave. Harran withdraws when he has either confirmed or failed to confirm what the documents contain. The soldiers withdraw when Harran signals it.

If cornered or spoken to directly, Harran says: *"When the city collapses in six months, tell me which principle saved it."* He means it. He is not performing. He believes the math and he believes the players have just confirmed it.

If attacked directly: he defends himself and does not hold back. He is not interested in dying to make a point.

**Session hook:** Harran now knows the players know. The players now know he knows. He has the resources of the Reckoning behind him and a clear timeline in his head. What he does next depends on what he read in those documents — and on what the players decide to do before he acts.

## End of Session

**What players now know:**
- The Dawnborn are the ritual — the ten people they have met and spoken to are Lux Anchors carrying redirected solar energy in their bodies
- Completing the ritual as designed means killing all ten simultaneously at the Ashring plaza
- Harran Lecht knows they know, and the Reckoning is not going to wait indefinitely for a voluntary solution

**What they carry forward:** They know something the Dawnborn do not, and every moment they spend with Sera or Tomas or Lira without telling them is a choice — to protect, to delay, or to decide on someone else's behalf.

**Session debrief question:** *"Do you have the right to decide someone else's fate, even to save thousands?"*
